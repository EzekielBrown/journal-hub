A wireframe, also known as a page schematic or screen blueprint, represents the structural framework of a website. Wireframes allow for the orderly arrangement of elements, intending to help to accomplish a particular goal.

Prototypes in product development are playable models built to prove the viability of a concept or process. This term is used in a variety of ways, including semantics, design, electronic engineering, and software programming. Prototypes are generally used to evaluate new designs to improve precision by system analysts and users.

Mockups, or mock-ups, are used in manufacturing and design to demonstrate designs, evaluate designs, promote products, and more. Prototypes are mockups that allow for testing of a design and provide at least part of the system's functionality.

Mood board is the arranging of images, materials, text, and other design elements into a format that represents the final design's style. You can use mood boards to create brand designs, product designs, and pretty much any other type of design project.

The pain point refers to a specific problem prospective clients of your business experience. It is important to recognise that pain points are simply problems. Problems with customers are just as diverse and varied as your prospects themselves.
